Matthew Cuschieri is a designer and artist from New Jersey. He is currently pursuing a BFA at RISD, majoring in graphic design and painting.
Previously he worked for Johnson & Johnson Brand Design Studio, Brian Belott and Ben La Rocco as a studio intern. He was recently invited to attend Anderson Ranch Arts Center, was featured in New American Paintings no. 152, the College Hill Independent, and exhibited in the Gelman Gallery (Memories and How We Hold Them) and Online Playroom (Love is in the Air).

Using the Awards' symbol of the anvil to inform the type and composition of the poster

With the anvil as a structural element the rest of the poster plays off the typical cartoony 'anvil fall.' When the anvil is larger, the text shrinks and the composition then changes. The custom type also plays off J&J's inhouse typeface, a requirement of the prompt. The typeface can easily stretch and squash. Its form is made through broken down shapes found in the anvil.

Collection Shelf

Tries to enact the coming and going of exploring a shelf. Each image structure provides info on the image itself aswell as a movable element to mimic the handling of an object. For a shelf to live on the web is to allow for users to take hold of its objects! The 3.js component takes a plane and allows each image to input its own video into the front plane. The plane has a backside which does not show the image, akin to a book page!
